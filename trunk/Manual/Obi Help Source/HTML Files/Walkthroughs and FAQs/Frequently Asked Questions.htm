Frequently Asked Questions

 

Q. Can I open multiple instances of Obi?

Yes, you can open as many instances as you wish. See the topic Starting Obi for starting an instance.

 

Q. Can I open multiple projects in an instance of Obi?

No, you can only open a single project. But you can open multiple instances of Obi and open a different project in each of them.

 

Q. Is the project file the same as the DAISY DTB?

No, the project is a XUK file, whereas the DAISY DTB is a collection of audio, navigation and synchronization files. A DTB is created when you export the project as DAISY.

 

Q. I do not have a daisy player, how can I hear the DTB?

You can sort all the audio files (with .wav/.mp3 extension) in alphabetic order and play them one by one in any player that supports wav/mp3 format.

 

Q. What is a unique ID?

Various schemes are available for identifying digital publications. In the DTB domain, the requirements for an identifier are simply to identify the publication in a manner that is highly likely to be globally unique. A major purpose of the uniqueness requirement is to prevent filename collisions among bookmark files.

To meet this base requirement, a simple DTB id scheme might be used. A DTB identifier under this scheme consists of a hyphen-separated string consisting of a two-letter country code drawn from [ISO 3166], an agency code unique within its country, and an identifier unique within the agency. For example, us-afb-x12345.

 

Q. Can I select multiple items in Obi?

You cannot select multiple items arbitrarily. But, when you select a section, all the phrases get selected, and if you select a section in TOC View, the subsections and their phrases get selected, too.

 

Q. Can I copy an item from one Obi project to another?

It is not possible to use the copy command across several instances of Obi. But, you can import audio files from the data folder of one project to another.

 

Q. What audio formats can be imported in Obi?

You can import audio files having WAV/RIF or MP3format. Before importing, Obi will alter the audio format of the audio file to match the audio settings (like channel and sampling rate) of your project.

 

Q. Can I import a DAISY DTB in Obi?

You can create a project by importing the structure and/or audio from a DAISY DTB, if the DTB conforms to DAISY 3 specifications.

 

Q. I have cleaned unreferenced audio, can I revert to previous state? What can I undo?

You cannot revert to an earlier state, once you have executed this command as it deletes all the audio data from the disk. However, the audio that is referenced in the undo record remains and can be reverted, if you perform redo operation(s).

 

Q. If I have not saved the project, and a crash occurs, what can I expect to loose?

All the data entered after the project was last saved will be lost. The audio data however, would remain on the disk till you re-open the project in Obi, but it would not be referenced in the project file. You can choose to auto-save to minimize the risk of loosing important data.

 

Q. Is there any Auto Save option in Obi?

Yes, you may configure Obi to save the changes automatically after some fixed amount of time. You may also choose to auto-save after each recording. These settings may be modified using the Project preferences tab of the preferences dialog.

 

Q. Can I hide the Content View?

No, the Content View cannot be hidden.

 

Q. When can I use blank phrases?

Blank phrases act as placeholders for audio content. You may use them for leaving a space for some audio content while recording.

 

Q. What if I do not mark any phrase of a section as a heading phrase?

If you do not assign a heading role to any phrase of the section explicitly, the first phrase will be considered as heading phrase.

 

Q. If the heading phrase is not the first phrase of the section, would the sequence of playing the phrases of the section change?

Obi will play all the phrases of the sections one by one from the first to the last. So, the audio from the heading phrase will be played after the audio from the preceding phrases have been played.

 

Q. Why is silence phrase marked as Unused?

Silence phrase is marked as Unused as it is used only for editing and should not appear in the DTB.

  

Q. How do I check for duplication of page numbers?

Duplication of page numbers is checked automatically before exporting, when you give the Export as DAISY command. But, you can also check manually if you wish to do so.

 

Q. How can I check for the correctness of the sequence of page numbers?

You can only check the sequence manually.

 

Q. Is there any auto correct option for page numbers?

No, Obi will point out the duplicate page numbers while exporting the project as a DAISY DTB, but you have to correct them manually.

 

Q. Can I select audio from multiple phrases?

No, arbitrary multiple selection is not allowed in Obi, and the same is true for the audio contained in phrases.

 

Q. What types of books can be produced in Obi?

Obi can produce digital talking books conforming to DAISY 3 and DAISY 2.02 standards.  

 

Q. How can I change my microphone volume?

Your microphone volume can be changed via the system settings. The steps given below illustrate the process of changing the microphone volume on Windows XP:

  1. Click Programs->Accessories->Entertainment->Volume Control on the Start Menu. Alternatively, you can double-click on the volume icon on the taskbar.
  2. Click Advanced Controls on the Options menu.
  3. Move the mic in volume slider up and down for increasing and decreasing the volumes respectively.

 

Q. What is preceding silence audio?

It is a phrase that holds a bit of audio for silence in your recording environment as it may differ from place to place. A silence phrase is used for phrase detection while auto-splitting phrases.

 

Q. How do I apply phrase detection with different set of values?

When you choose to apply phrase detection, a dialog box opens showing the parameters determined from a silence phrase. If Obi is unable to find a silence phrase, the dialog box shows the default parameters. You may enter your set of parameters in this dialog box, if you wish to use different ones.

 

Q. In which formats can Obi projects be saved using “Save Project As”?

The project can only be saved as a copy of the current project. To know how to save, see Saving project in different location.

 

Q. What is the difference between the commands Delete Unused Data and Clean Unreferenced Audio?

Delete unused data deletes all the data items (sections or phrases) that are marked as Unused from the project, and this command can be undone, whereas Clean Unreferenced Audio removes the audio data that is not referenced in the project from the disk and this command cannot be undone.

 

Q. What is the difference between zoom and audio zoom?

Zoom magnifies or shrinks everything that is visible in the work area of the Obi screen, whereas audio zoom scales the waveforms corresponding to all the visible phrases of the section horizontally.

 

Q. Why doesn’t the audio play on navigating?

The option Play on navigate may be turned off. To turn it on click Transport->Navigation->Play on navigate on the menu.

 

Q. What is play with elapse back?

If you are playing a piece of audio at a faster rate and want to hear something just heard again at a normal speed, press E. Obi will go back a certain unit of time and start playing at the normal speed.

 

Q. What is New project from import and how is it different from New Project?

New project from import allows you to import the structure and/or audio from a DAISY book or an xhtml file, whereas New project will create a blank project only and you have to create the structure manually.

 

Q. How can I clear the list of recent projects?

The submenu of File->Recent Projects has a Clear List option at the end of the list of recently opened projects. Click on it to clear the list.

 

Q. How can I paste a piece of audio inside a phrase?

First, play the audio of the phrase, and then pause at the position where you want to insert the audio. Then, use the paste command to paste the audio from the clipboard. For more information, see Editing audio.

 

Q. Are there any accessibility features for people with low vision in Obi?

Obi provides zoom and audio zoom for magnifying the elements of the Obi screen. To magnify or shrink all the elements of the Obi screen, press Ctrl+Alt++ or Ctrl+Alt+- respectively. To stretch or skew the waveform corresponding to a particular phrase, press Ctrl+Alt+Shift++ or Ctrl+Alt+Shift+- respectively. To further assist you while working, audio clues are provided. Obi also adjusts its colors for better visibility when the system is switched to high contrast mode.

 

Q. Can I mark or select the phrase for editing at a later stage during the time of recording?

Yes, you can do it by pressing F9. The current phrase will be marked as Todo, and Obi will create a new one next to it in the same section for recording. For more information on marking the structure while recording, see Create the Structure while Recording.

 

Q. How can I zoom the phrase? Can I zoom a particular phrase only?

You can zoom the waveforms corresponding to all the visible phrases of a section by selecting Audio Zoom in or Audio zoom out from the View menu.

  

Q. How can I convert DTB audio to MP3? Can I convert it into any other format?

You can encode an exported DTB to MP3 by selecting Convert audio to MP3 from the Tools Menu. You may also choose to encode audio files to MP3 at the time of exporting by simply checking the checkbox Encode to MP3.

This version of Obi does not allow you to encode in any other format.

 

Q. Can I make out the elapsed or remaining time during playback?

Yes, the Transport Bar has a Time Display control that shows the elapsed/remaining time during playback. You can also hear the time by pressing F8.

 

Q. What is the difference between selecting a phrase and selecting entire audio content of a phrase?

When you select a phrase clipboard of Obi will contain the phrase itself; whereas, if you select entire content of the phrase by pressing a single key A, the clipboard will contain the entire audio content present in the phrase, but not the phrase itself. This will result in different behaviors of the Paste command. 

 

Q. I encountered a package error code 2896 while installing Obi on Windows Vista. What should I do?

You must have the administrator privileges for installing Obi. Follow the instructions given below to install Obi on your system:

  1. Run the Command Prompt with administrator privileges.
  2. Go to the directory where the setup file is stored.
  3. Type setup.msi on the Command Prompt and press Enter.

The installer will start installing Obi.

 

Q. I want to perform some audio processing using Sound Forge. How can I do it?

Obi allows you to export audio of any phrase or section to an external file. See the topic Exporting audio to an External file for details.

 

Q. I have scanned images for a chapter of my book. The process of narrating the contents and recording in Obi is becoming very cumbersome. Can Obi change its skin like Windows Media Player to suit my requirements?

Obi comes with a pop-up toolbar that constitutes the controls for recording and structure mark-up. You can display it by clicking View->Show recording toolbar on the menu. Recording toolbar is separate from Obi’s application window, enabling the users to use it even when Obi is minimized. For more details, see the topic Recording toolbar.

 

Q. Can I configure shortcut key combination for an operation?

Yes, you can assign any key combination to Obi’s commands using the Keyboard shortcuts tab of the Preferences dialog.

 

Q. After applying phrase detection, I lost some useful piece of audio from the beginning of the phrase. What could have happened?

The option Retain initial silence must be turned off. You can turn this option on or off from the audio preferences tab of the Preferences dialog.

 

Q. I imported a very long audio file in a phrase and have lost most of the other phrases in my section. What could have happened?

When a phrase exceeds the current width of Content view, other phrases of the section are adjusted to match its length. To alter this behavior, you can delimit the width of Content view by checking the checkbox Fix Content view width in Project preferences.

Q. While importing a DAISY book, some errors occurred. Can I still import my book?

If the errors occurred due to discrepancies between the duration of audio content of phrases and their respective entries in the SMIL file, you can import your DAISY book. Simply, increase the Import tolerance using Project preferences.

 

Q. What is advanced recording mode?

Advanced recording mode lets the users to gear up the process of recording. You can enable or disable the advanced recording mode using Audio preferences. The options provided by this mode are described in the topic Live Recording.
