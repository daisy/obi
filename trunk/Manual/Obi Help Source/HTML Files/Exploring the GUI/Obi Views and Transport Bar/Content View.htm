Content View

 

The Content view unfurls the contents of a section. The name of the section is displayed on the top of the view as section label. Beneath the section label, the phrases of the section are exposed in multiple rows.

 

Each phrase contains some audio content. It has a label displaying its length (in time) and a waveform showing the visual representation of the audio contained in it.

 

When the audio corresponding to the phrase is being played, an audio cursor moves along the waveform, highlighting the playback position. If a portion of audio is selected in a phrase, the corresponding waveform is shown in inverted colors. A waveform can be scaled along the X-axis using audio zoom.

 

A phrase separator is present before and after every phrase acting as a boundary of the phrase.  You can press Ctrl+Left Arrow or Ctrl+Right Arrow to activate the strip cursor that moves from one phrase separator to another. Strip cursor is used for inserting a phrase in its position.

Content view has a horizontal scroll bar and a vertical scroll panel that lets the users to move the window horizontally and vertically respectively.

 

Just above the horizontal scroll bar, there is a tag displaying the section name of the section whose contents are currently being exhibited in the Content view. When a new section is brought in the view, the tag starts showing its name. The tag remains constant throughout. In other words, it is unaffected by scrolling.

 

The width of the Content view can also be made variable or fixed using the Project preferences tab of the Preferences dialog.

 

Show/Hide Content View

Content View is always visible. Since this is the main view of obi, it cannot be hidden.
