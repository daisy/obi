Elements of Obi

 

To better understand Obi, you must be familiar with the elements of Obi.

 

Elements

Description

Phrase

A single unit of audio content that represents a sentence, a part of sentence or even an expression.

Section

A structured unit containing phrases and subsections. It is a generic term that can represent a chapter, section, subsection or any other part of the book like table of contents etc.

Phrase separator

A boundary that separates one phrase from another in a section. It is also present at the beginning of the first phrase or at the end of the last phrase.

Strip cursor

A cursor that moves from one phrase separator to another in a single section.

Waveform

A visual representation of audio in a phrase.

Audio Cursor

The position from which the audio is being played. It is represented as a vertical line moving through the waveform horizontally.

Audio selection

A portion of audio selected from a phrase.

 

Containers

Obi stores its data in two elements, namely:

  • A phrase that holds a single unit of audio content of the DTB
  • A section that encapsulates a set of phrases that form its contents, and the name of the section.

 

These elements can be termed as containers.

 

Used / Unused Items

During the work flow, a situation may arise where you may want to hold some data that is not currently in use. There can be many reasons to hold such data. Like, you may not be sure whether some audio content should be deleted, or some part of it has to be retained.

 

The container elements of Obi can be marked Used or Unused. The ones marked as Used will appear in the DTB, whereas Unused ones will be available only in the project. Both used and unused items are available for editing in Obi.

  

Todo Phrases

In addition to the above, you can mark a phrase as Todo to highlight it for book editor. This feature is very helpful in scenarios like, narrator made some mistakes while narrating the contents of the book. Such phrases can be marked as Todo and can be attended later.
