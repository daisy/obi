Cutting, Copying, and Pasting the Sections

 

Obi maintains a clipboard of its own that enables it to hold the items that have been cut or copied.

 

Cut

You can cut a section by clicking Edit->Cut on the menu or pressing Ctrl+X.

 

Copy

Similarly, a section can be copied by clicking Edit->Copy on the menu or by pressing Ctrl+C.

 

If you have cut or copied a section in TOC View, its subsections will also be cut or copied.

 

But if the focus is in Content View, only the selected section will be cut or copied. All its subsections will be brought to its depth.

 

Paste

You can paste the section you have just cut or copied after the selected item by clicking Edit->Paste on the menu or pressing Ctrl+V.

 

The section from the clipboard will be pasted after the selected section at the same level. In this process, the subsections of the selected sections will not be disturbed and the section from the clipboard will appear after all the subsections.

 

For example, if we paste a section named Y after the section named X with two children A and B, the section Y would be pasted after section B and have the same level as section X.

 

Paste Before

You can also paste a section before a section by clicking Edit->Paste Before on the menu or pressing Ctrl+Alt+V. 

The section from the clipboard will be pasted before the selected section at the same level.

 

Paste Inside

In addition to the above, you can paste a section as a subsection of the selected section by clicking Edit->Paste Inside on the menu or pressing Ctrl+Shift+V. 

The section from the clipboard will be pasted as the last subsection of the selected section.

 

Remarks:

  • When you give a paste command for pasting a section from the clipboard, and the keyboard focus is on a phrase or a phrase separator, the paste position will be determined using the enclosing section of the selected phrase or phrase separator.
  • Paste Inside and Paste before commands are disabled in Content view.
