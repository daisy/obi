Fast Play

 

In the process of book production, many times, there may be a need for hearing the playback at faster rate; for example, while editing the book contents, or doing last minute inspection, etc.

 

The Fast play rate combo box on the Transport Bar lists various choices of increasing the rate of playback. Select a value depending on how much faster you want to hear the playback. For example, if you wish to hear the playback 1.5 times faster, select 1.5 from the combo box.

 

Generally, the pitch gets distorted when the fast play rate is increased or decreased. But Obi preserves the pitch across the faster and slower playback, giving a uniform feel to its users. The Audio tab of the Preferences dialog contains a checkbox Fast play without pitch change that enables you to activate or deactivate this feature.

 

Obi provides the following options for Fast Play:

·        Speed up

·        Slow down

·        Normal speed

·        Normal speed with elapse back

 

Speed Up

Increases the pace at which audio is being played. This command chooses the next value from the Fast play rate combo box and multiplies the normal speed of playback by that much times. For example, if you give this command when the combo box is showing 1.5, Obi will select the next value: 1.75.

 

To speed up the playback, click Transport->Fast play->Speed up on the menu, or press Ctrl++. Alternatively, you can use a single key shortcut: F.

 

Slow Down

Decreases the pace at which audio is being played. This command chooses the previous value from the Fast play rate combo box and multiplies the normal speed of playback by that much times. For example, if you give this command when the combo box is showing 1.75, Obi will select the prior value: 1.5.

 

To slow down the playback, click Transport->Fast play->Slow down on the menu, or press Ctrl+-. Alternatively, you can use a single key shortcut: S.

 

Normal speed

Returns the speed of playback to normal. The Fast play rate combo box will start showing the value: 1.

 

Normal speed with Elapse Back 

If the playback is in progress, and you want to hear something that just passed, you can do it by using this feature. Press a single key E quickly, and Obi will go back a small amount of time and start playing the audio at the normal rate (even if it was playing at a faster rate).

 

If you pause the playback before giving this command, the audio cursor will be moved backwards by the amount of the elapse back duration. Consequently, the Resume command will resume playback from the new position of the audio cursor. But if Obi is in the stopped state, the audio cursor will be placed a little before the end of the selected phrase.

 

The elapse back duration can be modified using the fields Operation and Duration in milliseconds in the Audio preferences tab of the Preferences dialog.
